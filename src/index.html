By taking part in Vibe 360 we want you to get the most well-rounded feedback from the respondents you choose so you can get the best out of the 360 process.

Now it’s time to choose your respondents, please take the time to select who you would like to ask for feedback and use the guidance notes throughout this process.

Submitting your respondents shouldn’t take longer than 15 minutes. Please take care to follow the instructions closely to ensure you get the well-rounded feedback that’s important to your continued leadership development at Capita.

HOW TO CHOOSE YOUR RESPONDENTS

Choosing who you will ask for feedback from is possibly the most important part of the Vibe 360 feedback process and should be given some time and attention prior to completing the survey.

There are two types of respondents Laws of Attraction ask for feedback when completing a Vibe 360 survey – those who give observational feedback and those who give reputational feedback:

  1. Observation feedback is from a respondent that has ‘seen you do it’. This is typically your line manager and direct report group.
  2. Reputational or perception feedback is from a respondent that might say ‘I think you should do that’. They are basing their answer on their perception of you or what they have observed from a distance.

HOW TO CHOOSE YOUR RESPONDENTS

We collect feedback from 6 groups of people. Line Manager is one person. Each of the remaining respondent groups need a minimum of 5 people, however you can have up to 8 in each group. This ensures that anonymity is kept in each of the groups delivered in your personal report.

All of the groups aside from ‘Others’ are compulsory, this ensures diverse and well-rounded feedback is collected which is integral to the Vibe 360 report.

Self: This is you

Line Manager: This is your immediate line manager. If you report into two managers, or have a dotted line into another, choose the manager who is the most appropriate to give you feedback.

Direct Reports: These are the people who report into you. If you don’t have five team members that report into you, choose other people that operate at a similar level that you work with, or maybe other people in your team.

Peers: This group is for your peers. People at a similar level in the business that work alongside you on a daily/weekly basis.

Peers 2: This respondent group is for peers who work outside your function or area of business. It’s important that this group reaches out to those people that span a cross section of the business.

Others: The ‘others’ section is optional. This section can be for whoever you wish. Some examples would be stakeholders, customers, suppliers or more senior people in the business, or a project group you’ve recently worked with. As long as the people you ask for feedback from makes sense you can be as diverse as you like in this group.

GOLDEN RULES FOR CHOOSING YOUR RESPONDENTS

Don’t just choose your mates... You may get a glowing report but it won’t really give you a balanced view that can help you develop and grow.

Don’t just choose people you don’t get along with. Focusing on all the people you may not have seen eye to eye with recently will not be beneficial as it will be too one sided. However, it is a good idea to ask some of your colleagues that have a different working style to you so that you can get a balanced opinion of both your strengths and your development areas.

Choose people with whom you have a relationship with... Lots of people use Vibe 360 as an opportunity to ask some of the senior people in the business for feedback. Whilst this can be useful (asking your bosses boss for example) asking the CEO whom you’ve met once might not be that useful in the long run.

When grouping the feedback one thing to remember is that the group has to ‘make sense’. Therefore, it’s not a good idea to ask a very junior person in your team that has been in the business for 3 months in the same section as three very senior people that you’ve worked with for 10 years.

Choosing your respondents wisely means that you will get a better quality report.
